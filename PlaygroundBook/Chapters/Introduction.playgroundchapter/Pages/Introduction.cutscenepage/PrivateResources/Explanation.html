A microcontroller is a small computer on an integrated circuit. A microcontroller usually includes a processor, memory and input-output (I/O) capabilities. As opposed to the microprocessors used in iPhones and Macs, microcontrollers are usually used in embedded systems, performing some specific task to enable a system to work. Where are they used?
Everywhere! Microcontrollers' low cost and small size allow them to be used in an extremely diverse variety of applications, from controlling power tools to controlling automobile engines. When you start a load of laundry in a washing machine, a microcontroller controls the cycle. When you pour yourself a cup of coffee with a digital coffee machine, a microcontroller is controlling the entire process. When you flip open your Airpods, a microcontroller detects the opening and initiates pairing with your iPhone.
